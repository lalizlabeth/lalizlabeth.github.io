Product Design

CharmBits

Summary

CharmBits are modular circuit wearables that teach children the basics of electronics. This project was created in UC Berkeley's Interactive Device Design in Fall 2014. Throughout the course of a semester, student teams work together to design an interactive device combining the fields of UI design, 3D modeling, and electronics. My team included Doug Cook, Ian Shain, and Kiyana Salkeld. I focused on the interface design, iOS implementation, and packaging.

Defining the Problem

Our first assignment required us to brainstorm 20+ ideas with sketches that met the following project requirements:

  • Must include custom 3D printed case or mechanism for your device
  • Must be interactive: include sensing and/or actuation circuitry
  • Must have some graphical user interface somewhere
  • Must communicate
  • Must pick a concrete user group
  • Must solve a real problem or improve their life in some meaningful way

    • After a number of rounds of brainstorming, we determined a few key areas of interest that our group wanted to explore. We tried to consolidate a few of our main interests, education and fashion, into one idea. We eventually looked into providing children with an educational way to explore circuits and electronics.
