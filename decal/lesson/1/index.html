Basic Principles: Repetition

The principle of repetition refers to reusing the same element throughout your design. Repetition can bring a sense of unity and consistency. Conversely, it can also create contrast and bring out certain elements within a design. For example, using a set group of typefaces and colors in a slide deck, creates unity in a presentation.

Activity: One-Line Logo

You’ve been asked to submit an identity design for the 2016 Olympic Games in Rio. The initial sketch of your logo must be composed from a single, unbroken line. Once you’ve placed your pen or pencil down on the paper, you can’t take it off the page until the logo is complete. Don’t go back for corrections! - adapted from Creative Workshop.
