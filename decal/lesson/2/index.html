Lesson 2

The Design Process

Graphic design is more than making pretty posters and eye-catching logos. In technical terms, graphic design is defined as:

The art and profession of selecting and arranging visual elements—such as typography, images, symbols, and colours—to convey a message to an audience.

It’s not that simple. Graphic design is about understanding people to make a difference in their everyday lives.

Case Study: USA Today Redesign

1. Define the Problem

Before you start brainstorming/sketching, make sure you understand the problem you are trying to solve. If a client tells you, "We need a new logo and color palette for our restaurant," you should ask "Why?" Questions like the following can help you narrow the scope of your ideas.
