Ancient Apple Product Packaging

Apple packaging wasn't always small and compact. They used to pride themselves in having extravagant boxes that contributed to the experience of having an Apple product. After much criticsm, they finally decided to condense their packaging to be as minimal as possible.

Why is packaging important?

Packaging is a necessity. It is primarily used to protect, contain, and identify products. Here are some attributes of good packaging.

Identification

Packaging should capture the brand of the company. Coca-Cola is a good example. It's really easy to identify a Coca-Cola bottle from a Pepsi bottle. Color is a really easy way to emphasize the brand of any type of packaing.

Another example of great packaging is Tiffany & Co. Consumers are able to identify a Tiffany box because of their signature blue and ribbon.

Packaging should help you identify the function of a product. This packaging below, for example, is a little confusing. It's hard to tell that this box is for wipes because the photo emphasizes food instead.

Functionality

Good packaging is also multifunctional. Sometimes the actual product is merged with the packaging. The paint bucket below allows you to put a paint roller on the lid.

Another example of multifuncitonal packaging is this pizza box

Navigation

You should be able to identify a group of "products" on a wall without much effort. For example, at hair salons, they have walls of hair products. It's easy to group brands together because they have different, unique packaging

Environmental

Packaging should also be environmentally friendly! The importance of sustainibility in design is rising. Check out this designer's amazing disappearing project here.

Transportation

Packaging is also used for transportation. Today, most products are bought online, so packaging must be optimized for movement. Birchbox is a subscription-based company that sends personalized make up samples. They are well-known for their small shippable packages that fit a lot of content.
